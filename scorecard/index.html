Key workforce risk factors for CBA

For organisations in the Civic Leader band, risk shifts from "closing capability gaps" to defending leadership margin. The four risks below derive from public-signal analysis of CBA, peer movement, and the regulatory environment.

REGULATION

CBA is publicly positioned as an Australian-first AI policy publisher (Our Approach to Adopting AI, Dec 2025). This raises regulator expectation. APRA CPS 230 operational resilience requirements and the EU AI Act's extraterritorial reach mean leadership margin must be actively defended — being public sets the bar that regulators benchmark against.

REPUTATION

Aug 2025 incident — CBA reversed a decision to replace ~45 customer service roles with a voicebot after public backlash and FSU intervention. Response demonstrated maturity but flagged that public AI failures cost more for leaders. The Feb 2026 $90M Future Workforce announcement (with explicit job-mapping language) reads as an explicit reputational repair.

ROBUSTNESS

30,000+ employees trained leaves ~25,000 untrained (~45% of workforce). To maintain Evident AI Index #1 in Talent Development, CBA needs full coverage by FY27. The Grow Your Career portal addresses individual mapping but enterprise-wide consistency between front office, ops, and corporate is the next frontier.

RESOURCE

Lost Andrew McMullan (former Chief Data & Analytics Officer) to Westpac in May 2025 — CEO Matt Comyn publicly noted the "executive raid." Active poaching pressure from peers means CBA must deepen its AI bench beyond named executives. Ranil Boteju (Chief AI Officer) is an asset; succession depth is the question.

Methodology & framework

The Solaris model is a synthesis of four respected AI maturity frameworks, calibrated against the largest publicly available enterprise AI dataset and validated through external rating systems. CBA's Q2 2026 score was independently verified against the Evident AI Index 2025.

Source frameworks

AIRI — AI Singapore
PRIMARY

4 pillars × 9 dimensions × 4 readiness levels (AI Unaware → Aware → Ready → Competent). Solaris adopts AIRI's Organisational Readiness pillar (4 dims) as its workforce backbone, plus Business Use Case and Adoption Measurement.

ESI ThoughtLab
BENCHMARK DATA

Driving ROI Through AI (2020). Survey of 1,200 firms across 12 industries and 15 countries (combined revenue $15.5T). Source for 4-stage maturity benchmarks and ROI correlations used in dimension weighting.

Element AI
FRAMEWORK

AI Maturity Framework (5×5 grid: Strategy, Data, Technology, People, Governance × Exploring → Transforming). Source for stage progression model and dimension interaction.

Evident AI Index
VALIDATION

Annual ranking of 50 banks across 70+ indicators (45% Talent / 30% Innovation / 15% Leadership / 10% Transparency). CBA's 2025 ranking (#4 globally, #1 APAC) used to calibrate Solaris score.
