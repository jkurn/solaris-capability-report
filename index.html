Key workforce risk factors for Apex

To develop a scorecard, Solaris investigates whether existing capability development, governance, and stakeholder activities demonstrate appropriate AI workforce readiness. Based on our analysis of Apex Financial Group, we have identified the following key risk factors:

REGULATION

Australian financial services is on the leading edge of AI regulatory scrutiny — APRA's CPS 234 update and the EU AI Act's extraterritorial reach create rising compliance pressure. Apex maintains baseline alignment but lags peers on published responsible-AI frameworks. Failing to demonstrate workforce-wide AI literacy creates regulatory exposure.

REPUTATION

Organisations with similar profiles have faced reputational risk from AI rollouts that bypassed workforce training (model misuse, biased outputs, employee resistance). Apex's lack of a formal AI capability program means line managers and frontline staff have no shared baseline for responsible AI use — a known precursor to public incidents.

ROBUSTNESS

Per ESI ThoughtLab, 79% of firms with negative AI ROI lack a measurement system. Apex's annual report references AI investment six times but never quantifies returns or adoption. Without a measurement layer, the AI program is invisible to the CFO and indefensible at budget review.

RESOURCE

Apex's ~145 visible AI professionals look impressive but are 1/5 the talent density of ESI's Leader cohort (avg. 715). The bigger gap is workforce-wide: 88% of AI overperformers train non-data-scientists in AI; only 2% of underperformers do. Apex shows the underperformer pattern. Hiring won't close this — capability development will.

Methodology & framework

The Solaris model is a synthesis of four respected AI maturity frameworks, calibrated against the largest publicly available enterprise AI dataset and validated through Build Club's hands-on engagements with 200+ organisations across APAC.

Source frameworks

AIRI — AI Singapore
PRIMARY

4 pillars × 9 dimensions × 4 readiness levels (AI Unaware → Aware → Ready → Competent). Solaris adopts AIRI's Organisational Readiness pillar (4 dims) as its workforce backbone, plus Business Use Case.

ESI ThoughtLab
BENCHMARK DATA

Driving ROI Through AI (2020). Survey of 1,200 firms across 12 industries and 15 countries (combined revenue $15.5T). Source for 4-stage maturity benchmarks and ROI correlations.

Element AI
FRAMEWORK

AI Maturity Framework (5×5 grid: Strategy, Data, Technology, People, Governance × Exploring → Transforming). Source for Solaris's stage progression model and dimension weighting.

O'Reilly & Gartner
VALIDATION

O'Reilly AI Adoption in the Enterprise (2022, n≈2,800) and Gartner Government AI Survey (n=53). Used to validate adoption-stage distributions and challenge prevalence by sector.
